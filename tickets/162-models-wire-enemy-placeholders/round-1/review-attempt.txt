**The blocking defect is grounding.** Enemy meshes are placed at `world y = halfHeight` (renderer.js:3175) and minions at `world y = 0.5` (renderer.js:3348), because THREE.js centers the procedural geometry at the origin — so the procedural primitive's base lands on the floor. But `normalizeLoadedModel` grounds the loaded model to the **host's local** y=0, and the model is a child of that already-lifted host. Net result: every loaded model's feet land at `world y = halfHeight`, floating each enemy/minion ~50% of its own height above the ground (grunt +0.5, skirmisher +0.3, miniboss +0.9, spawner +0.6, minions +0.5). This directly violates the AC "sits on the ground (feet at the entity's y, not centered through the floor)." The `renderer-model-fit.test.js` grounding check only inspects the model in its own local space, so it never catches the lifted-host float.
