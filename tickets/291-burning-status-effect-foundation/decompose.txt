**Rationale for the split (dependency order):**

1. **01 — Server helpers/state**: pure `applyBurning`/`isBurning` + `burningUntil` state + exports + unit tests. Smallest verifiable unit, no dependencies. Mirrors `applySlow`/`isSlowed`.
2. **02 — Server damage tick**: the actual DoT — a `updateBurning()` pass wired into `runGameLoopTick`, interval-gated damage via `damagePlayer` (free godmode immunity) and `damageEnemy`, snapshot exposure of `burningUntil`, plus the ticket-mandated tests for tick damage + expiry on players AND enemies, godmode immunity, and refresh. Depends on 01.
3. **03 — Client flame animation**: flickering flame visual on players and enemies driven by the broadcast `burningUntil`, mirroring the existing slow-indicator machinery. Depends on 02 exposing the field.

All three are `code` verification: 01/02 concern functions, server state, timing, values, and an error/immunity path; 03 mixes "flame exists/animates/cleans up" structural checks with look — and since no card/enemy lights anything on fire yet (296/297/298 land later), it can't be confirmed in a happy-path screenshot run, so it must be read from the diff.
